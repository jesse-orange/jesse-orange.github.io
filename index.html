Self-introduction   

About Me

Hi, I’m Jixi (Jesse) Gu, a high school student from Nanjing No.1 Middle School, China. I spend much of my time thinking about how logic and emotion coexist — in games, in code, and in the quiet rhythm of everyday life.

My name, 缉熙 (Jixi), comes from The Classic of Poetry , where it means “to preserve brightness.” My mother chose it to remind me that clarity grows not from certainty, but from reflection. My father balanced that ideal with humor and a nickname — “Orange,” because, as he likes to say, “life should have both sweetness and weight.”

Between Algorithms and Intuition

I have been playing Xiangqi (Chinese chess) since I was four, under the guidance of Grandmaster Zhang Guofeng. Over time, I realized that chess — like life — isn’t about winning or losing, but about connection. That belief led me to invent two new chess variants, Hybrid Chess and Four-Nation Chess, which blend Chinese and Western systems. They were born from curiosity, but taught me empathy: a game only matters when people enjoy it together. I am also fascinated by consciousness and computation — how reasoning systems can never fully replicate human fragility or creativity. My exploration of this idea led me to design small coding projects and even write essays on how logic meets emotion in everyday decision-making.

What I Believe

I believe creativity is a form of clarity. Whether I’m writing, playing music, or solving problems, I look for that “moment in between” — where precision meets intuition, and structure meets spontaneity. To me, that’s what makes life bright — not perfection, but the chance to feel fully human.
