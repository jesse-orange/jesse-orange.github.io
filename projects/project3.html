Spatial Narrative and Critique of Reality in the Film "Breaking"

Research Objectives

  • To analyze how the three types of spatial narrative (geographical, communicative and psychological) in "Breaking" collectively construct a narrative structure for social critique.
  • To reveal how the film uses spatial narrative to expose the suppression and neglect of individual identity and dignity by the American social system.

    • Research Questions

  • How do geographical spaces (e.g., welfare office, bank) symbolize the protagonist's identity rupture and social exclusion?
  • How do communicative spaces (e.g., media, police negotiation) reflect the individual's silencing and lack of agency within the system?

    Research Methods

  • Spatial narrative theory analysis:
    Using narrative space theory to interpret the symbolic meanings of geographical, communicative, and psychological spaces in the film.
  • Close reading and scene deconstruction:
    Analyzing cinematography and dialogue in key scenes (e.g., bank hostage, phone calls) to uncover the film's social critique.
