Sign Language Recognition

Research Objectives

The understanding of sign language routinely depends on visual inputs, and automatic sign language recognition can use pattern recognition techniques to realize the translation of this visual-gestural language into the normal spoken or written language. The higher the sign language recognition accuracy is, the smoother the communication between deaf and dumb people and the social world will be. Therefore, our study aims to further achieve the better sign language recognition performance.

Research Methods

A new binary black-winged kit optimization algorithm based feature selection method is employed to search for and obtain better feature subsets in the feature space, and has an effect on the improvement of sign language recognition accuracy.

Sign Language Recognition

Experiments

  • K Nearest Neighbor with the wrapper-based feature selection was applied to data classification.

  • Three sign language datasets from Kaggle were used in experiments.
