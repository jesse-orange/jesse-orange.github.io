Philosophy Project

Art, Machines, and Existence: The Case of Zima Blue

Scroll to explore

Research Objectives

  • To explore how Zima, the robot in the 14th episode, “Zima Blue”, of the 2019 Netflix series Love, Death & Robots, pursues self-awareness through artistic creation and intertwines this journey with philosophical themes such as death and free will.
  • To analyze whether the robot demonstrates a human-like process of "self-discovery" in this journey, and how such a process redefines the relationship between machines and humans.
  • To examine the philosophical connections and conflicts between AI and humans from perspectives of existentialism, free will, creativity, and death, while discussing the concepts of robotic freedom and autonomy.

    • Research Questions

  • Art and Self-Discovery:
    What role does artistic creation play in Zima’s self-discovery in “Zima Blue”? What kind of meaning does he pursue through art, and can this pursuit be regarded as a form of human-like “self-actualization”?
  • Freedom and Programming:
    How does Zima’s creativity transcend his original functional programming? In the discussion of whether a robot can surpass its assigned functions and tasks, what role does free will play?
  • Death and Return to Zero:
    Zima ultimately chooses to “return” to his original blue pool, and abandons self-awareness and artistic creation. How does this process reflect a philosophical understanding of death? How is this concept of “death” expressed within an AI framework, and how does it contrast with the human concept of death?
  • Human-Machine Relations:
    Through Zima’s artistic creation and self-discovery, how can we understand the connections between humans and machines in terms of emotion, creativity, freedom, and death? Do these relationships suggest a new existential perspective?

    • Research Methods

  • Textual Analysis:
    Conduct a detailed analysis of key plot points in “Zima Blue”, focusing particularly on Zima's artistic creative process, his reflections on life and death, and the final scene of his choice to return. These narrative elements are crucial for exploring the themes of art, self-discovery, and death.
  • Philosophical Framework:
    Employ philosophical theories such as existentialism, free will, and the philosophy of death to construct a theoretical framework for interpreting the series' events and Zima's actions.
  • Comparative Study:
    Perform a comparative analysis with other films and media, exploring AI and artistic creation to investigate how different works portray the philosophical issues of self-awareness, free will, and artistic creation related to robots.
