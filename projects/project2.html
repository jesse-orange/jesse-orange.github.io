Posthumanism and Biotechnology: The Philosophy and Ethics of Self-Transcendence

Delving into the intricate relationship between technology and human existence in modern society by philosophical thought

Explore the Research

Research Objectives

  • To explore how posthumanism alters human self-identity and capabilities through biotechnology.
  • To investigate the philosophical implications of using drugs, genetic editing, and other means for self-transcendence, and whether such transcendence can lead to human evolution or a form of degradation.
  • To analyze the ethical consequences of self-transcendence: Should such capabilities be encouraged or restricted? What are their implications for society and the individual?

    • Research Questions

  • Self-Transcendence and Human Limitations:
    In the face of human limitations, how do we understand and evaluate self-transcendence achieved through pharmaceuticals, gene editing, or other biotechnological means? Can these methods genuinely achieve transcendence, or do they merely mask inherent human limitations?
  • External Means and Self-Transcendence:
    Can artists, philosophers, or even ordinary individuals transcend their cognitive and emotional limits by using drugs, technology, or other external aids? Can this type of "transcendence" be viewed as a disruption or a form of self-negation of human nature?
  • Ethics and Morality:
    From an ethical standpoint, is the use of biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, and other means for self-transcendence justified? Might this pursuit of self-transcendence exacerbate social inequalities or lead to the creation of a new "superior human" class? How should the application of such technologies be regulated?
  • Existentialism and Freedom:
    Within the posthumanist framework, how do human free will and self-realization interact with the intervention of biotechnology? Is this self-modification a act of free choice, or is it a destiny driven by technological determinism?

    • Research Methods

  • Case Studies:
    Analyze real-world or science fiction examples, such as artists using drugs or virtual reality technologies to "transcend" themselves and achieve bursts of inspiration. These instances can help illuminate the philosophical phenomenon of "self-transcendence," such as philosophical responses to issues of drug abuse, gene editing, or neuroenhancement.
  • Philosophical Framework Analysis:
    Integrate Nietzsche's theory of the Übermensch and posthumanist philosophical thought with modern biotechnology to explore how contemporary technology challenges the human "self." Particular focus will be placed on how technology provides new modes of self-expression while simultaneously creating a crisis in human self-understanding.
  • Ethical Discussion:
    Drawing on contemporary ethics, technology ethics, and social justice theory, discuss the potential social problems arising from self-transcendence technologies.
